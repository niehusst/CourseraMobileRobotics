How do you know when you need to introduce another behavior in order to act well in an environment?

Obstacles that are points or circles can be dealt with by simple switching behavior.

Convex, non-convex, and labrynth obstacles require an additional sliding mode in order for the robot to be able to get around obstacles that require a robot to go in a direction that is not even slightly in the direction of the goal.
To get around these obstacles, wall/boundary following is necessary.
